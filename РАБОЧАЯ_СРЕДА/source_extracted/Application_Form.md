## Technical Details
Guidance note:
- Applicants (in particular those applying for Design Grant) are encouraged to provide information to the best of their knowledge and based on reasonable estimations. Where specific information is not yet available, applicants should indicate how the requested funding will be used to generate, refine, or validate this information during project implementation.
- The guiding questions are provided for reference only. Applicants are not required to respond to each question individually, but should ensure that sufficient information is provided to enable a comprehensive assessment of the proposal.

| 3.1 Climate context and background of the project (max. 700 words) |
|---|
| Guiding questions: What are the observed/experienced climate hazards (i.e. heatwaves, floods, droughts, wildfires, sea-level rise) in the proposed intervention location? What is the current projection of future hazards? Cite evidence or relevant literature.  What grey infrastructure is exposed to the above-mentioned climate hazards? What impacts have already been observed, or expected to be seen over time?  What are the likely consequences to the above-mentioned infrastructure over time (both physically and financially) under the above-mentioned projection? For more information related to climate hazards, vulnerability and exposure, please refer to here.   Please also describe the scale and severity of vulnerability affecting the beneficiary groups, defined as the populations and users who depend on or are served by the affected infrastructure or ecosystem. |
| 3.2 Project description (max. 1500 words) |
| Please provide the proposed project outcome, outputs, and activities that will be undertaken here. Please describe how the proposed activities will address the barriers described in section 3.1, why this set of interventions was selected and how the interventions can help unlock the needed support in a sustainable manner. Also identify trade-offs of the selected interventions, if applicable.  Please address the following: How the proposed intervention reduces the current and future climate risks  How the intervention will reduce the exposure or vulnerability of the target infrastructure to the identified climate risks, including, where possible, number of direct beneficiaries, share of the affected population (%) (i.e. proportion of the total population served by the infrastructure or located within the project’s area of influence) How the proposed NbS works, what are the roles of the green (and grey) components Peak capacity: Indicate expected capacity (e.g. water retention, etc.) What’s the Technical Readiness, i.e. concept only, feasibility study done or implementation-ready Pilot readiness: status of site identification  Site description: Provide brief physical & ecological context, land access, permitting and stakeholder engagement.  Attach design drawings, site maps, modelling results (if available)  For proposals requesting Design grant, please describe the objective and expected outcome of the underlying project as much as possible and also how the proposed studies will help design the underlying intervention.   This section should be consistent with the proposed logframe and budget. |
| 3.3 Implementation arrangement (max. 1000 words) |
| Please provide a description of the implementation structure, outlining legal, contractual, institutional and financial arrangements among the consortium lead, other consortium members and beneficiaries.   Please explain the governance arrangements set to oversee and guide project implementation. Provide a composition of the decision-making body and oversight function.   For each consortium partner, please provide their names, organization type, country(ies) where they are legally registered and where they are permitted to operate, their relevant project experience, areas of expertise, and proposed roles in the project.  Please articulate the specific expertise each partner contributes, particularly in relation to infrastructure development, NbS, and climate adaptation. While previous experience in integrating NbS in grey infrastructure is not required, it is essential to explain how consortium partners can utilize their previous experience, expertise and network to ensure the success of the design and piloting of the proposed interventions.   Applicants are encouraged to attach a separate document—using tables or diagrams—to illustrate the composition and structure of the consortium, as well as the flow of funds. |
| 3.4 Alignment with national climate and sustainable development policies and frameworks (max. 500 words) |
| Please describe how the proposed interventions align with national climate and development frameworks such as National Determined Contributions (NDCs), National Adaptation Plans (NAPs), sectoral development plans, as well as Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs). Please explain the expected impacts, including co-benefits on environmental, social (including health), economic and gender sensitive development.   Please provide information on land ownership and/or tenure rights and access rights, where applicable, and demonstrate stakeholder consent.  Please also provide information on the consultations conducted (and plans for further consultation) with national and local authorities on the proposed intervention. While it is not mandatory at application stage, evidence of endorsement/no-objection from relevant authorities for the design/pilot of the proposed intervention will be required before any proposals can be approved by NBS-IA.  For proposals in Central Asia, applicants are encouraged to refer to the recommendations provided in the guidance report on Nature-based Solutions in the United Nations Economic Commission for Europe (UNECE) region, as well as other relevant country Environmental Performance Reviews (EPRs). |
| 3.5 Innovation contribution (max. 500 words) |
| Innovation is defined as technical, institutional, financial, delivery/governance, or evidence-based innovation. The solution does not need to be “new” in absolute terms but must produce demonstrable added value in the local context. This could include, but not limited to, innovative designs of NbS integration with grey infrastructure, the scale and scope of integration, the use of technology, data, and materials, financial innovation as well as implementation arrangement. Also includes first time use in locally specific context. |
| 3.6 Potential for scalability, replicability and additionality (max. 500 words) |
| Please address the following if applicable Scalability: Clear pathways for expanding the solution to different sites and assets, beyond the implementing country and or region.  Replicability: Evidence that the solution can be adapted to other countries or regions with minimal modifications. Additionality: Proposals must explicitly demonstrate and justify why the project requires NBS-IA grant support and would not proceed under business-as-usual conditions or existing financing mechanisms, demonstrating true additionality.   Proposals are encouraged to demonstrate synergies with existing and/or planned initiatives in the same location and/or sector by other institutions, including but not limited to government agencies, local organizations, multilateral development banks, and international organizations. |
| 3.7 Financial management and financing plan (max. 500 words) |
| In this section, please provide the financial management and financing plan for 1) the design/pilot phase with grant from NBS-IA (and co-finance from consortia partners), and 2) the financing plan for scaling up investment from investors and capital providers at scale for the implementation of the underlying infrastructure development.   For the design/pilot phase using grant (and co-finance) from NBS-IA, please provide a short description of:  Use of grant funding: Breakdown of how the requested grant will be allocated across key activities and partners Co-financing and additional resources: Sources of co-financing, and what activities will the co-finance contribute to Articulate the financial management plan/system, roles and responsibilities of consortium lead and members.  Elaborate on whether any procurements will be carried out using the grant from NBS-IA, if so, the estimated amount and information on the goods and services that will be procured, including grey component.  For the implementation and up-scaling of the underlying infrastructure beyond pilot, please provide a financial and investment plan at asset level. This could include: What is the estimated investment needed (and implementation period) for the implementation and up-scaling of integrating NbS into the target infrastructure development (both green and grey components)? What financial instruments are foreseen to be used?  Who are the potential investors for the implementation and up-scaling of integrating NbS into the target infrastructure (both green and grey components)? What are the plans to involve them throughout the design/pilot phase? Are there any challenges in securing their financing? How would the consortium using the grant from NBS-IA to overcome these challenges? |
